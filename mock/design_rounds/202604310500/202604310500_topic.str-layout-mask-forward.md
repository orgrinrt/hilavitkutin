# Topic: Str forwards its mask constants to StrLayout

**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Status:** TOPIC phase
**Task:** #72 slice (follow-up to round 202604290000 after arvo round 202604310000)

## Context

Round 202604290000 moved `Str` to a `bitfield!`-generated
`StrLayout`. Str kept its own `RUNTIME_MASK` / `ID_MASK` constants
(and a legacy `CONST_ORIGIN_FLAG = 0`) — duplicating layout
information that StrLayout now owns authoritatively.

Arvo round 202604310000 just landed per-field `<field>_MASK`
constants on `bitfield!`-generated structs. StrLayout now exposes
`origin_MASK`, `reserved_MASK`, `id_MASK` directly.

## Decisions

- **Drop `Str::CONST_ORIGIN_FLAG`**. No consumer uses it; `is_const()`
  and `is_runtime()` cover the check.
- **Forward `Str::RUNTIME_MASK = StrLayout::origin_MASK`** (single
  `Bits<32, Hot>` binding; name preserves consumer-facing clarity
  while the definition moves to the layout).
- **Forward `Str::ID_MASK = StrLayout::id_MASK`**.
- **Persistence**: switch `Bits::<28>::new(value)` call sites to
  `value.into()` where the context is non-const (arvo round
  202604310000 added `From<u64> for Bits<N, Hot>` per-N).

## Out of scope

- Exposing StrLayout as part of the public API. The type stays
  "internal layout carrier" per its doc comment; the Str mask consts
  are the consumer-facing surface.
- The rest of the #72 sweep.
