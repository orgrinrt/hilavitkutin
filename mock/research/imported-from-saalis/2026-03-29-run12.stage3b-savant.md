## My Disagreement

I disagree with Expert B's conclusion that the polling model question
is settled by "we are writing a Rust library." Expert B writes:

> "We are not writing a protocol specification for multi-language
> implementation. We are writing a Rust library."

This is factually true today and strategically wrong forever. The wire
format (W1-W18) is necessary but not sufficient for interoperability.
A C implementor reading only the wire spec will build a working decoder
but a broken state machine, because the state machine's invariants
(reentrancy prevention, output ordering, arena lifetime) are not in the
wire spec. They are in the polling model. Expert B concedes this at the
end: "if the project ever produces a formal protocol specification
document, that document should describe the abstract interface as
two-phase." That concession proves my point. The two-phase model IS
the specification. The four-method model is one optimization of it.
Designing the Rust trait as four methods and then saying "the spec
will be two-phase" means the spec is written after the implementation,
which is backwards.

But I am done fighting this. Expert B's QUIC analogy (quinn uses four
methods, quiche uses two phases, both implement the same wire protocol)
is real. The wire protocol is the contract. I accept that the Rust
implementation uses four methods. I maintain that the specification
document, when written, describes two phases. Expert B already agreed
to this. The disagreement is resolved.

What I will NOT accept is Expert B's claim that cross-transmit/event
ordering "is not observable to the protocol's correctness." It is not
observable to THIS protocol's correctness because the protocol has
no cross-type ordering invariants. But the specification should not
preclude protocols that do. The two-phase model preserves causal
ordering as a free property. The four-method model discards it. That
is a capability loss, even if no current mechanism needs it. I note
it. I move on.

## Section 2: Final Corrections Accepted (Hard Constraints Only)

### Correction 1: Supersaturation Controls TransferSessions, Not Chunks

Expert A identifies that chunk-based replication budgets are
fundamentally limited by the small chunk size (1170 bytes) and the
heartbeat-tick granularity. A P1 blob at 0 replicas in Idle mode at
10 chunks per tick: 10 * 1170 / 5 = 2,340 bytes/second. A 2 GB blob
takes ~9.9 days at Idle pace. This is too slow.

Expert A's correction: the supersaturation rate should control how
many concurrent TransferSessions to open, not how many individual
chunks to send. Each TransferSession runs its own LEDBAT flow. The
supersaturation controls parallelism, not per-session throughput.

This is correct. My chunk-based model was a holdover from thinking
about replication as a per-tick work item instead of as a continuous
transfer. TransferSessions with bao streaming are the actual transfer
mechanism. The supersaturation rate determines how many sessions to
open. Each session runs at LEDBAT pace. The aggregate throughput is
governed by LEDBAT, not by an artificial chunk budget.

Accepted. The replication triad becomes:
- Priority queue: WHAT to replicate (most_under_replicated).
- Probabilistic roll: WHO replicates (BLAKE3 per-peer deduplication).
- Supersaturation rate: HOW MANY concurrent sessions to open.

### Correction 2: Two New f64 Violations in My Stage 2b

Expert B identifies two fixed-point violations I introduced:

**Violation 1: LEDBAT WiFi correction.** I wrote "widens by
(1 / wifi_quality^2)." This is f64. Expert B provides the Q16.16
integer reciprocal-square with a 16x cap. Accepted.

**Violation 2: WiFi scar decay formula mismatch.** My prose says
"wifi_quality 0.60 -> 1 day." My formula gives 1 + (0.60 * 6) = 4.6
days, not 1. Expert B is right: my prose and formula disagree. The
formula is correct. My prose examples are wrong.

The intended behavior curve: shorter decay for worse WiFi quality.
The formula 1 + wifi_quality * 6 gives:
- 0.60 quality -> 4.6 days (not 1 day as I wrote).
- 0.75 quality -> 5.5 days.
- 0.85 quality -> 6.1 days.
- 0.95 quality -> 6.7 days.

This curve is wrong. Worse WiFi should produce shorter decay (the scar
is more likely environmental). The formula gives longer decay for worse
WiFi. The variables are inverted.

Corrected formula: days = 1 + (1 - wifi_quality) * 6.
- 0.60 quality -> 1 + 0.40 * 6 = 3.4 days.
- 0.75 quality -> 1 + 0.25 * 6 = 2.5 days.
- 0.85 quality -> 1 + 0.15 * 6 = 1.9 days.
- 0.95 quality -> 1 + 0.05 * 6 = 1.3 days.

In Q8.8:

```
fn wifi_scar_decay_days(wifi_quality_q8_8: u16) -> u16 {
    // days = 1 + (1 - wifi_quality) * 6
    // In Q8.8: one_minus_q = 0x0100 - wifi_quality_q8_8
    let one_minus_q = 0x0100u16.saturating_sub(wifi_quality_q8_8);
    let days_q8_8 = 0x0100u16.saturating_add(one_minus_q.saturating_mul(6));
    days_q8_8 >> 8
}
```

At 0.60 (Q8.8: 0x99): one_minus_q = 0x0100 - 0x99 = 0x67. days_q8_8
= 0x0100 + 0x67 * 6 = 0x0100 + 0x026A = 0x036A. days = 0x036A >> 8
= 3. Good. Short decay for bad WiFi.

At 0.95 (Q8.8: 0xF3): one_minus_q = 0x0100 - 0xF3 = 0x0D. days_q8_8
= 0x0100 + 0x0D * 6 = 0x0100 + 0x004E = 0x014E. days = 0x014E >> 8
= 1. Good. Very short decay for near-normal WiFi (borderline events
disappear fast).

## Section 4: The Final Shape of the Crystal

The crystal after Run 12, Stage 3b. Six layers, three new structural
features, and every bond specified.

### 4.1 The Replication Crystal

The replication system is a crystal growth mechanism with three
interlocking components:

**Component 1: Priority Queue (WHAT).** most_under_replicated()
selects the blob with the highest deficit. Deficit is weighted by
priority (Expert A's extension): P1 at 3.0x, P2 at 2.0x, P3 at 1.0x,
P4 at 0.5x. The queue determines the growth face: the crystal grows
at its thinnest face first.

**Component 2: Probabilistic Roll (WHO).** BLAKE3(blob_id, my_dk,
epoch) produces a deterministic per-peer decision. The roll probability
is deficit / (known_holders + 1), computed in Q16.16 fixed-point. The
roll prevents thundering herd: not every peer holding the blob
initiates replication. The roll is coordination-free.

**Component 3: Supersaturation Rate (HOW MANY).** The growth rate
controls concurrent TransferSessions (Expert A's correction), not
chunks. Each TransferSession runs bao streaming at LEDBAT pace. The
supersaturation formula:

```
effective_supersaturation = (deficit / target) * priority_weight
sessions_to_open = max(1, (base_rate * effective_supersaturation) >> 16)
sessions = min(sessions_to_open, metabolic_max_sessions)
```

Base rates: Background = 1, Idle = 3, Active = 2, Stress = 1. These
are TransferSession counts, not chunk counts. Minimum is always 1
session. Maximum is governed by the metabolic state.

**Gate:** REPLICATION_READY = filter_reconciliation_complete AND
convergence_front >= Front2. The gate prevents replication decisions
on stale filter data. After the gate opens, Expert A's feedback-
controlled warmup ramp brings sessions online gradually.

**Path:** Replication starts on the relay path (which is already
established after convergence) and migrates to the direct path when
hole-punching succeeds.

### 4.2 The Diagnostic Crystal

The diagnostic system is a three-input measurement that classifies
every connectivity event.

**Input 1:** direct_health (EMA of direct-path SACK ratios).
**Input 2:** relay_health_normalized (Expert C: raw relay SACK /
wifi_quality, Q16.16 division, clamped to [0, 1.0]).
**Input 3:** wifi_quality (CapacityRatio, fed via ResourceMeasurement
input per Expert B).

**Output:** PathDiagnosis, 8-way match on three boolean thresholds
(0.75 in Q16.16). Must persist for 3 consecutive ticks before
triggering automated responses (Expert B's temporal stability).

**Automated responses:**
- RelayFault: failover to warm standby relay.
- NatExpired: send NAT keepalive. Do NOT trigger convergence.
- DestinationFault: scar the destination (structural, 180 days).
  Trigger PARTITION_ANN after partition_threshold seconds if
  remediation fails (Expert C's extension).
- WifiTransient: attenuate scar generation. Widen scar threshold
  to 30 consecutive misses.
- WifiPlusRelay: attenuate scars AND failover relay.
- WifiPlusDirect: attenuate scars AND renew NAT keepalive.
- LocalLinkDown: halt outgoing traffic, enter degraded mode, delay
  convergence.
